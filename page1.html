Sainsbury's Customer Choice Modelling

This initiative aims to comprehensively analyze the decision-making process and influential factors guiding customers' product selection, specifically bagels at Sainsbury's supermarkets across multiple store locations.

Approach

To reach these goals, a deep dive into the data was used to understand its characteristics and effecting factors. Multiple approaches were used including splitting the data into low and medium volumes of products sold. Additional, to further investigate a zone wise segmentation of supermarkets was conducted along with a brand wise segmentation. A range of supervised algorithms were Meticulously scrutinized and compared including random forest, XGboost, decision trees and neural networks. From these ML Algorithms XGboost was found to perform the best being most accurate and having the best weighted F1score. 
2

Methodology 

To achieve our objectives, an extensive data analysis was conducted to comprehend its characteristics and influencing factors. The dataset was stratified into low and medium product sales volumes. Additionally, segmentation was applied to supermarkets based on geographical zones and product brands. A comprehensive evaluation of supervised algorithms, including Random Forest, XGBoost, Decision Trees, and Neural Networks, was meticulously carried out. Among these machine learning algorithms, XGBoost emerged as the top-performer, exhibiting the highest accuracy and the most favorable weighted F1 score.
3

Insights

Analysis of the segmented data revealed distinct patterns. In the low-volume category, the top predicted item selections were consistent, while in the high-volume category, a different set of items emerged as top choices. Further examination of brand-wise segmentation showcased Brand A and Brand C as having their respective top item selections. Moreover, while top predicted items aligned across zones, variations were noted in the least predicted items, underscoring regional differences.
4

Impact 

These insights empower Sainsbury's to refine their product assortment, ensuring constant availability of high-demand items while phasing out less popular ones. Furthermore, the probability distribution aids in assessing customer price sensitivity, enabling optimal pricing strategies that maximize revenue and strike the right balance between price and demand. Employing choice modeling techniques, Sainsbury's can enhance their marketing strategies, delivering more targeted campaigns to customers. In addition, understanding customer preferences allows for streamlined supply chain management, aligning inventory levels with demand, thus minimizing stockouts, improving inventory efficiency, and enhancing operational effectiveness.
